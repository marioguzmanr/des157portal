10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills
February 9, 2023

The article "10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills" presents a collection of ten photographs that can be used in the classroom to teach students how to analyze images and think critically. Each photograph is accompanied by a set of discussion questions that encourage students to examine the details and symbolism in the image, as well as to make connections to their own experiences and the broader world.



The photographs range from a simple image of a girl on a swing to more complex scenes such as a protest in Tahrir Square during the Arab Spring. The photographs are meant to be used as a starting point for class discussions and to help students develop their critical thinking skills.



The article emphasizes the importance of visual literacy in today's world, as we are constantly bombarded with images in advertising, social media, and news media. By teaching students to analyze and interpret images, educators can help them become more informed and discerning consumers of visual media. Overall, the article provides a useful resource for educators looking to incorporate visual literacy into their curriculum, and for anyone interested in developing their own skills in close reading and visual thinking.

Best practices for Modals/Overlays/Dialog Windows
February 7, 2023

Modals, Overlays, Dialog Windows, and Pop-ups play an important role in User Interface Design. These elements are used to provide users with additional information, feedback, or prompts while they interact with an application. It is important to design these elements correctly to ensure a positive user experience.



Modals should be non-intrusive and easily dismissible, and they should only contain essential information such as error messages, confirmation prompts, or important notifications. Appropriate visual cues, such as background color, text color, and font size, should be used to ensure that the modal stands out clearly from the background and is easily readable.



Accessibility is another important factor to consider when designing modals. This means ensuring that the modal can be easily used by users with disabilities, such as those who are visually impaired or have motor impairments. This could include features like large, clear text, keyboard navigation, and alternative text for images. By keeping these factors in mind, modals can improve the user experience by providing essential information in a non-intrusive and accessible manner.

Best practices for form design: 20 tips to boost form usability
January 31, 2023

The article "Best Practices for Form Design" highlights the importance of form design in user experience (UX) and provides tips on how to create effective forms. The author states that forms are crucial for tasks such as sign-ups, logins, and checkout processes, and therefore, it's essential to get them right. The first step is to keep the form short and simple. A short form is more likely to be completed, and a simple form reduces the risk of errors and confusion.
The article also suggests using clear and concise language, consistent formatting, and appropriate input fields. Additionally, providing helpful feedback and error messages is crucial for a smooth user experience. The article also highlights the importance of mobile optimization, as more and more users access the web on their mobile devices. Finally, the article emphasizes the importance of testing and iterating forms to ensure that they are user-friendly and effective. In conclusion, good form design can greatly improve the user experience and increase conversions.
